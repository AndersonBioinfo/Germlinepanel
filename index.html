Germline gene panel

A Germline Gene Panel refers to a specific set of genes that are analyzed for variations in the germline DNA of an individual. The germline DNA is inherited from both parents and is present in the reproductive cells (sperm and egg). This is in contrast to somatic DNA, which makes up the non-reproductive cells in the body.

Hereditary Cancer gene panel

A Hereditary Gene Panel is a specific set of genes that are analyzed to identify genetic variations associated with hereditary conditions or diseases. This type of genetic testing focuses on examining the germline DNA, which is the DNA inherited from both parents and is present in reproductive cells (sperm and egg).
